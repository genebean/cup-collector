✈️ Using While Traveling

This is the scenario the app was built for. Here's how a typical trip goes.

You land in Kyoto. Before you've even picked up your luggage, you open Cup Collector from your home screen. The map loads and immediately centers on Kyoto — and you can see two orange pins nearby. Those are cups you still need from this region.

You tap one of the orange pins. It's a You Are Here cup from 2022. You don't own it yet. Below the cup's image, the app lists three nearby Starbucks locations. You tap Maps → next to the closest one, and Apple Maps opens with turn-by-turn walking directions.

You walk over, find the cup on the shelf, and it's beautiful. You buy it. Back in the app, you open the cup and tap ✓ Mark as Owned. The button changes instantly. And on your spouse's phone — wherever they are — the map updates within a second. The orange pin turns green. They don't need to refresh anything.

Later that afternoon you're near a second Starbucks. You tap Search and type "Kyoto" — the other cup you need comes right up. You find it, buy it, mark it owned. Two new green pins on the map.

That evening you're curious about tomorrow's city. You open Browse, type "Osaka" in the search bar, and see which cups are from there and whether you have them. You make a mental note — there's a Been There from Osaka you've never seen in person.

The whole workflow takes about ten seconds per cup. No spreadsheet, no syncing, no "wait, do we already have this one?" The app just knows.
