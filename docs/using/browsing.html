🔍 Browsing

The Browse screen is your main window into the cup catalog — filter by series, country, or scope, search by name, and manage your collection.

Browse screen

Tap Browse in the bottom navigation. The full catalog appears as a scrollable list. The header shows a pill like 42/1,947 owned › — your owned count out of the total — which is also a tap target to open the Collection Stats screen.

Filters

The header contains dropdown filters and a row of status chips, all working together:

Row 1 (always shown):

  • Series — filter to a single series (You Are Here, Been There, Discovery Series, etc.). Series that include both mugs and ornaments appear as two separate entries — for example, Been There for mugs and Been There Ornaments for ornaments.
  • Country — filter to cups from a specific country (US, Canada, and Mexico are pinned to the top for quick access)

Row 2 (shown when applicable):

  • Scope — filter by cup type: Cities, States, Countries, or Themed. Only appears if your catalog includes cups with multiple scopes.
  • Collection — filter by sub-collection such as "Campus Collection". Only appears when cups with sub-collection tags exist in your catalog.

The status chips below the dropdowns let you narrow by ownership:

  • All — show everything
  • Still Need — only cups you don't own yet
  • Already Have — only cups you own
  • Near Me — sorts unowned cups closest to you first (only appears when location is active)

The search bar at the top filters by cup name, country, or series as you type. Tap the × inside the box to clear it. All filters combine — for example, "You Are Here" series + "Japan" country + "Still Need" gives you only the Japanese You Are Here cups you still need.

When you tap the ⚠ needs replacing warning on the Stats page, Browse opens with a special Needs Replacing filter active. A gold banner at the top of the filter area shows this filter is on — tap ✕ on the banner to clear it and return to the normal view.

Filters persist when you navigate away If you tap a cup to view its detail and then tap Back, the Browse screen restores your filters and scroll position exactly as you left them. Filters reset when you navigate to a different main tab.
Near Me doesn't filter — it sorts Tapping Near Me reorders the list with unowned cups closest to your position shown first. All cups still appear; nothing is hidden. Useful when you're in a city and want to know which needed cups are nearby.

Quick ownership toggle (swipe)

Owner only Swipe-to-own is only available to household owners. Viewers see the list but cannot swipe.

Swipe any cup row left to reveal a quick action button. What the button does depends on the cup's current state:

  • Mark Owned (green) — cup is not yet owned; tap to add it to your collection
  • Unmark (orange) — cup is owned and in good condition; tap to remove it from your collection
  • Replaced ✓ (green) — cup is owned but flagged as needing replacement; tap to clear the flag

For cups with multiple versions (variants), the swipe targets the newest version (highest numbered variant). To toggle a specific version, open the cup's detail screen instead.

Tap anywhere on the card to close the action button without triggering it.

Collection preferences and what's shown

Browse only shows cups from the series and item types your household has chosen to track (set in Settings → What I Collect). Unowned cups from excluded series or types are hidden. Owned cups always appear regardless of preferences.

Collection stats

Tap the owned/total pill in the Browse header (e.g. 42/1,947 owned ›) to open the Collection Stats screen. It shows progress rings for mugs and ornaments, series bars, and a country breakdown you can drill into by region and city. See Collection Stats for full details.

Cup types: mugs, ornaments, and themed cups

The catalog contains several kinds of cups:

  • City mugs — the standard location cup, placed as a map pin at its city
  • State / country mugs — cover a whole state or country rather than a specific city; shown as map pins at the region centroid
  • Themed cups — special edition cups (e.g., Star Wars, Discovery Series) not tied to a specific location; labeled Special Edition on their detail screen and shown in city popups on the map rather than as their own pins
  • Ornaments — miniature collectible versions of some location cups; tracked alongside mugs and toggleable in collection preferences

In the Browse list, themed cups show a small "themed" badge next to their name. On the cup detail screen, the subtitle identifies the scope: State Cup, Country Cup, or Special Edition. City cups and ornaments have no scope prefix.

Cup detail

Tap any cup in the Browse list to open the Cup Detail screen. The Browse thumbnail and the detail hero both show the image of the newest version (highest-numbered variant) when multiple versions exist. This shows:

  • The cup's image (or a letter placeholder if no image is available)
  • City, country, series, year, and scope label
  • Any catalog notes (variants, limited editions, etc.)
  • External reference links — HobbyDB and/or a more info link to the source site (shown only when the data exists)
  • Nearby Starbucks stores where this cup might be sold
  • My Notes — per-household notes about this cup (see below)
  • A condition card and ownership button (owners only)

Tap the cup image to view it full-size. Tap anywhere outside it or swipe down to dismiss.

My Notes

Each cup has a My Notes section on its detail screen — a free-text area for your household's personal notes about that cup (separate from the catalog notes that come from the import). For example: where you saw it in the wild, a note about a specific variant, or a reminder to look for it in a particular city.

  • All household members can read the notes
  • Only owners can edit them — type in the text area, then tap Save. Tap Discard to undo unsaved changes.
  • Notes are per-household — if you belong to multiple households, each has its own note for each cup

Marking a cup as owned

Viewers cannot mark cups as owned The ownership button is only visible to household owners. If you don't see it, your account has Viewer access — ask the household owner to update your role in PocketID.

There are three ways to mark a cup as owned:

  • Swipe in the Browse list — swipe left on any cup row to reveal a quick action button. Tap Mark Owned to add it, or Unmark to remove it. Fastest when working through a list.
  • Mark as Owned button — open the Cup Detail screen and tap "✓ Mark as Owned". To remove, tap "Remove from Collection".
  • Acquired here — on the Cup Detail screen, tap Acquired here next to a nearby Starbucks. This marks the cup as owned and records the store where you got it in one tap.

Any of these changes take effect immediately and all other household members' apps update within a second or two — no manual syncing needed.

Condition tracking

Once a cup is marked as owned, a Condition card appears on its detail screen. By default the status shows In good condition.

Tap Edit to update:

  • Check Needs replacing if the cup is cracked, faded, or worn
  • Add an optional note explaining why (e.g. "cracked lid")
  • Tap Save to commit, or Cancel to discard

Cups flagged as needing replacement show a gold Needs Replacing badge in the Browse list and a gold map pin — easy to spot without being confused with cups you don't own at all.

Recording where you got it

When you mark a cup as owned, the Condition card shows a list of nearby Starbucks locations. Tap Acquired here next to the store where you found the cup. The store name and address are saved to the cup's record and shown on the detail screen for future reference.

Uploading your own photo

Once a cup is owned, a 📷 camera button appears in the bottom-right corner of the cup thumbnail inside the version card. Tap it to choose or take a photo — your photo replaces the catalog image for that cup in your household's view.

To remove your personal photo and revert to the catalog image, tap the 🗑️ trash button that appears in the bottom-left corner of the thumbnail. Both buttons are owner-only.
