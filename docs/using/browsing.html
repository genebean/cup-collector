📋 Browsing & Searching

The Browse and Search screens let you explore the full cup catalog, filter by series or country, and mark cups as owned.

Browse screen

Tap Browse in the bottom navigation. You'll see the full catalog as a scrollable list, with a count in the header showing how many cups you own out of the total.

Filter chips across the top let you narrow the list:

  • All — every cup in the catalog
  • Still Need — only cups you don't own yet
  • Series chips — filter to a specific series (You Are Here, Been There, etc.)

The search bar at the top filters by city, country, or series as you type.

When location is active, the list sorts so unowned cups near your current position appear first — useful when you're in a new city.

Search screen

Tap Search in the bottom navigation. Type a city name, country, or series — results appear instantly. Each result shows whether the cup is owned or still needed.

Cup detail

Tap any cup in the Browse or Search list to open the Cup Detail screen. This shows:

  • The cup's image (or a letter placeholder if no image is available)
  • City, country, series, and year
  • Any notes (variants, limited editions, etc.)
  • A condition card (owners only, once the cup is marked as owned)
  • Nearby Starbucks stores where this cup might be sold
  • An ownership button (owners only)

Tap the cup's image to view it full-size. Tap anywhere or press Escape to dismiss.

Marking a cup as owned

Viewers cannot mark cups as owned.
Only household owners see the ownership button. If you don't see it, your account is set to viewer-only.

On the Cup Detail screen, tap "✓ Mark as Owned". The button changes immediately and the other household member's app updates within a second or two — no manual syncing needed.

To remove a cup from your collection, tap "Remove from Collection" on a cup that's already owned.

Condition tracking

Once a cup is marked as owned, a Condition card appears on its detail screen (visible to owners only). By default the status shows In good condition.

Tap Edit to update the condition:

  • Check Needs replacing if the cup is cracked, faded, or otherwise worn
  • Add an optional note explaining why (e.g. "cracked lid")
  • Tap Save to commit, or Cancel to discard the draft

Cups flagged as needing replacement show a Needs Replacing badge on the Browse list so they're easy to spot.

Recording where you got it

When you're at a Starbucks and mark a cup as owned (or shortly after), the Condition card shows a list of nearby Starbucks locations. Tap Acquired here next to the store where you found the cup. The store name and address are saved to the cup's record and shown on the detail screen for future reference.

Uploading your own photo

Once a cup is owned, a camera button appears in the corner of the hero image. Tap it to choose or take a photo — your photo replaces the catalog image for that cup. This is useful when the catalog photo doesn't match the specific variant you have, or when no catalog photo exists.

Tap the hero image at any time to view it full-size.
